Correction Normalization (V9) local_std

Why Normalize Corrections?

V7 correctly identified noise locations but predicted 0.4m corrections where 70m was needed. Huber loss with delta=1.0 gives identical gradient magnitude for errors above 1m. The model has no incentive to predict 70m over 2m. V8's attempt to raise delta failed because the 95th percentile (0.652m) was below the min_delta floor.

V9 normalizes corrections by local surface variability (local_std). A 70m spike in variable terrain (local_std=5m) becomes 14 std devs. A 0.3m spike in flat terrain (local_std=0.05m) becomes 6 std devs. At inference, predictions are multiplied back by local_std. The QGIS diff layer (V9 minus V7) confirms corrections now reach up to 32m larger.

Key Observations

  • V1/V2 (Synthetic, 10 tiles): Both oscillated around 48% val accuracy, near random. Adding uncertainty gave negligible improvement.
  • V3 (Ground truth, shoal loss): First real data model. Too conservative at 0.6% noise detection.
  • V4 (Manual 2x weight): Overcorrected to 96.8% noise, low confidence (0.455).
  • V5 (No class weights): All-seafloor collapse. 67% accuracy = seafloor proportion.
  • V6 (Auto weights): 3.3% detection -- classifications followed survey boundaries. Root cause: uniform_filter bleeding nodata into features.
  • V7 (Boundary fix): Masked local stats eliminated boundary artifacts. 34.8% noise detection, 72% accuracy, 0.825 confidence. First model with real spatial noise detection. But corrections too small (0.4m where 70m needed).
  • V8 (Huber delta): Delta from data's 95th percentile (0.652m) hit min_delta floor. Identical to V7.
  • V9 (local_std norm): Corrections normalized by local surface variability. Classification unchanged (same features/weights). Correction magnitudes increased by up to 32m vs V7. 12,823 auto-corrections (vs 11,790 in V7). Still not fully recovering clean surface, but substantial improvement.
  • Data pair investigation: 3 new survey pairs (FL river, AK coast, Norfolk river) all had near-zero grid-level noise. CUBE gridding likely already rejected outlier soundings. These pairs would dilute training signal without adding noise examples. Rejected from training.
  • Persistent overfitting: Val loss diverges after ~5 epochs across all GT versions. All training data from same Seward area. Geographic diversity with grid-visible noise remains the top priority.

Next Steps

  • Find diverse training data: Need survey pairs where noise is visible in the gridded BAG surface, not just the point cloud. Quick litmus test: subtract surfaces in QGIS and look for scattered spikes.
  • Validate V9 corrections: Compare V9 correction magnitudes against known noise cells in QGIS to quantify improvement over V7.
  • Sounding density feature: Per-cell sounding count from point cloud data would be the strongest noise discriminator. Single-hit cells are almost certainly noise.
  • Adaptive Huber delta: Options 2/3 documented in losses.py for future implementation once diverse data is available.
